Recipe for Hyderabadi Chicken Dum Biryani

Ingredients

For Marinating Chicken:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste

For Cooking Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • Water for boiling
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • Salt to taste

For Biryani Masala:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish)

Instructions:

Marinating the Chicken:

  1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours in the refrigerator.

Cooking the Rice:

  1. Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt, bay leaf, green cardamom pods, and cloves.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water.
  4. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain and set aside.

Preparing the Biryani Masala:

  1. In a large heavy-bottomed pan, heat oil over medium heat.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown and crisp. Remove half of the fried onions for garnish.
  3. Add green chilies, chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, and garam masala powder to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil separates.
  4. Add marinated chicken and cook until it changes color and is partially cooked.
  5. Add chopped mint and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  6. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Drizzle some ghee over the rice.

Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

  1. Place a heavy lid or seal the edges of the pan with wheat dough to create a tight seal.
  2. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes. This process is known as "dum cooking" and allows the flavors to meld together.

Final Assembly:

  1. Remove the pan from heat and let it sit for a few minutes before opening.
  2. Gently fluff the Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani with a fork.
  3. Garnish with the reserved fried onions and saffron-soaked milk.
  4. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or a side salad.

Enjoy your homemade Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani!
